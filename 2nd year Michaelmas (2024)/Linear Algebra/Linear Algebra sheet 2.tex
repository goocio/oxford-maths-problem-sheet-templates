\question%1
Let $A$ be an $n \times n$ matrix over $\mathbb{C}$. Use triangular form to show that the trace of $A$ equals the sum of the eigenvalues, where a multiplicity $m$ eigenvalue is counted $m$ times. Show also that an analogous statement holds for the determinant and the product of the eigenvalues.



\question%2
Calculate the minimum and characteristic polynomials of the following matrices:
\begin{parts}
\part%2a
$\begin{pmatrix}
	1 & 1 & 0 \\
	-9 & -4 & 1 \\
	-3 & 3 & 2
\end{pmatrix}$,

\part%2b
$\begin{pmatrix}
	-2 & -3 & -3 \\
	-1 & 0 & -1 \\
	0 & 1 & -1
\end{pmatrix}$,

\part%2c
$\begin{pmatrix}
	2 & 1 & 0 \\
	0 & 2 & 0 \\
	0 & 0 & 2
\end{pmatrix}$
\end{parts}



\question%3
\begin{parts}
\part%3a
Find two $2 \times 2$ matrices which have the same characteristic polynomial but which are not conjugate.

\part%3b
Find two $3 \times 3$ matrices which have the same minimal polynomial but which are not conjugate.

\part%3c
Find two $4 \times 4$ matrices which have the same minimal polynomial and the same characteristic polynomial, but which are not conjugate.

\part%3d
What is the smallest value of $n$ for which there are two non-conjugate $n \times n$ matrices which have the same minimum and characteristic polynomials and eigenspaces of equal dimension?
\end{parts}



\question%4
Let $T: V \to V$ have minimum polynomial $m_{T}(x)=x^{m}$. Show that we have strict inclusions \[
	0 \subset \ker T \subset \ker(T^2) \subset ... \subset \ker(T^{m-1}) \subset \ker(T^m)=V.
\]



\question%5
Decide whether or not the matrix \[
	\begin{pmatrix}
		1 & 6 \\
		3 & 5
	\end{pmatrix}
\] can be diagonalised over the field:
\begin{parts}
\part%5a
$\mathbb{R}$

\part%5b
$\mathbb{C}$

\part%5c
$\mathbb{Q}$

\part%5d
a field of characteristic 2 (i.e. $1+1=0$)

\part%5e
the field $\mathbb{F}_{7}$ of integers modulo 7.
\end{parts}



\question%6
Is the matrix \[
	B=\begin{pmatrix}
		0 & 0 & -1 \\
		0 & -1 & 0 \\
		1 & 0 & 1
	\end{pmatrix}
\] diagonalisable over $\mathbb{C}, \mathbb{R}$, or $\mathbb{F}_{3}$?

\end{questions}

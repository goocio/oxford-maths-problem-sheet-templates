\question%1
Show that the ideal in $\mathbb{Z}[x]$ generated by 2 and $x$ is not principal.



\question%2
Which ideals $I$ in $\mathbb{Z}$ have the property that if $x^{n} \in I$ for some $n$ then $x \in I$?



\question%3
Describe the irreducible elements of the following rings
\begin{parts}
\part%3a
$\mathbb{C}[x]$,

\part%3b
$\mathbb{R}[x]$,

\part%3c
the ring $\mathcal{O}$ of holomorphic functions on $\mathbb{C}$.
\end{parts}



\question%4
Let $\phi: R \to S$ be a ring homomorphism.
\begin{parts}
\part%4a
Show that if $I$ is a prime ideal in $S$ then its preimage $\phi^{-1}(I)$ is prime in $R$.

\part%4b
Is the preimage of a maximal ideal maximal? Give a proof or counterexample.
\end{parts}



\question%5
Consider the ring \[
	\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-5}]=\{a+b \sqrt{-5}: a, b \in \mathbb{Z}\}.
\] We define the norm function $N: \mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-5}] \to \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$ by \[
	N(a+b \sqrt{-5})=a^{2}+5 b^{2} .
\]
\begin{parts}
\part%5a
Using this function, find the units of the ring, and show that 2 is an irreducible element.

\part%5b
Find two inequivalent factorisations into irreducibles of 6, and deduce $\mathbb{Z}[\sqrt{-5}]$ is not a UFD.
\end{parts}



\question%6
Let $R$ be an integral domain containing a field $K$. Explain how $R$ may be viewed as a vector space over $K$. Show that if $R$ is finite-dimensional as a vector space over $K$, then $R$ is a field.



\question%7
Let $\mathbf{k}$ be a field, and let $\mathbf{k}[[x]]$ denote the ring of formal power series over $\mathbf{k}$, that is power series $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_{n} x^{n}$ where $a_{n} \in \mathbf{k}$, but where we do not require the radius of convergence to be positive. We multiply series in the usual way.
\begin{parts}
\part%7a
Find the units of $\mathbf{k}[[x]]$.

\part%7b
Show there is a unique maximal ideal in $\mathbf{k}[[x]]$.

\part%7c
Is $\mathbf{k}[[x]]$ a Euclidean domain?
\end{parts}
